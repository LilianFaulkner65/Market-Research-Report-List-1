<p><p>Bio packing films are sustainable alternatives to conventional plastic wraps, primarily categorized into three types: PLA films, starch-based films, and other biopolymers. PLA films, sourced from corn starch or sugarcane, are biodegradable and offer excellent transparency and mechanical strength. Starch-based films, made from natural starches, are compostable and often used for packaging fresh produce. Other bio films include those made from cellulose, proteins, or other plant materials, providing diverse applications while minimizing environmental impact. Together, they contribute to reducing plastic pollution.</p></p>
