<p><p>The emergency contraceptive pills (ECPs) market primarily features two types: Levonorgestrel and Ulipristal Acetate. Levonorgestrel, commonly known as Plan B, is a progestin-based pill that is most effective when taken within 72 hours after unprotected intercourse. Ulipristal Acetate, marketed as Ella, is a selective progesterone receptor modulator that can be taken up to 120 hours after intercourse, offering a wider window of effectiveness. Both options provide critical support for preventing unintended pregnancies and are accessible in various markets globally.</p></p>
