<p><p>Sodium Hyaluronate, a key ingredient in skincare and medical applications, is sourced through two primary methods: Microbial Fermentation and Animal Tissue extraction. Microbial Fermentation involves bioengineering bacteria to produce hyaluronic acid, offering high purity, scalability, and sustainability, making it ideal for cosmetics and pharmaceuticals. Animal Tissue extraction derives hyaluronic acid from rooster combs or other animal sources, but it carries risks of impurities and ethical concerns. Both methods cater to diverse market needs, with fermentation dominating due to its efficiency and safety.</p></p>
