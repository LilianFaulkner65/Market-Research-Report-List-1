<p><p>The magnetoresistance sensor market features various types, including Giant Magnetoresistance (GMR) Sensors, which utilize significant changes in resistance in response to magnetic fields, commonly used in hard drives. Anisotropic Magnetoresistance (AMR) Sensors operate through resistance variation based on magnetization direction, benefiting applications like automotive and industrial sensing. Tunnel Magnetoresistance (TMR) Sensors leverage quantum tunneling for higher sensitivity, suitable for advanced data storage. Thin-Film Magnetoresistance (TFR) Sensors employ thin layers to detect magnetic fields, primarily used in consumer electronics and magnetic position sensing.</p></p>
