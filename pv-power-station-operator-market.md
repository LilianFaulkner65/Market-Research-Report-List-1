<p><p>PV power station operators manage two primary market types: on-grid and off-grid systems. On-grid PV power stations are connected to the utility grid, allowing them to sell excess electricity back to the grid, benefiting from net metering and incentives. They rely on grid stability for energy supply. In contrast, off-grid PV power stations operate independently, serving remote areas without grid access. They typically require battery storage for energy supply during non-sunny periods, catering to unique local energy needs.</p></p>
