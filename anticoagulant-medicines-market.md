<p><p>The anticoagulant medicines market is divided into two primary segments: injectable and oral anticoagulants. Injectable anticoagulants, such as heparins and direct thrombin inhibitors, are used for quick action in hospital settings, particularly for patients undergoing surgery or at risk of deep vein thrombosis. Oral anticoagulants, including vitamin K antagonists and direct oral anticoagulants, are preferred for long-term management of conditions like atrial fibrillation and venous thromboembolism, offering convenience and ease of administration for outpatient care.</p></p>
