<p><p>Distributed Fiber Optic Sensors (DFOS) are increasingly used in the power and utility market for monitoring infrastructure. The two main types are single-mode and multimode fibers. Single-mode fibers, with a smaller core, enable long-distance signal transmission with minimal loss, making them ideal for extensive utility networks. In contrast, multimode fibers, which have a larger core, support multiple light paths and are suited for short-distance applications, often used in localized monitoring systems. Both technologies enhance real-time data acquisition and enhance operational efficiency.</p></p>
