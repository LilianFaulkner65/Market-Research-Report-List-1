<p><p>The Backside Illuminated (BSI) CMOS sensor market is primarily segmented into two types: Passive Pixel Sensor (PPS) CMOS and Active Pixel Sensor (APS) CMOS. PPS CMOS sensors utilize passive pixel structures, which are simpler but generally lower in performance, making them suitable for basic imaging applications. In contrast, APS CMOS sensors incorporate active pixel structures, enabling enhanced image quality, higher sensitivity, and better noise performance, making them ideal for advanced imaging needs in smartphones, cameras, and other optical devices.</p></p>
