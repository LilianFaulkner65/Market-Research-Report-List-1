<p><p>Silicon epitaxial reactors are essential for producing high-quality silicon layers on substrates for semiconductor applications. The market comprises several types, including Metal-Organic Chemical Vapor Deposition (MOCVD) and Molecular Beam Epitaxy (MBE). MOCVD utilizes metal-organic compounds to deposit thin films, ideal for optoelectronic devices. MBE, on the other hand, employs molecular beams for precise atomic layer deposition, suitable for high-performance electronics. Other methods include hydride vapor phase epitaxy (HVPE) and quantum-dot epitaxy, catering to specific applications and materials needs.</p></p>
