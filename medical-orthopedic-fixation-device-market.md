<p><ul><li>External Fixation Device</li><li>Internal Fixation Device</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The medical orthopedic fixation device market encompasses two primary types: external fixation devices and internal fixation devices. External fixation devices are applied externally to stabilize fractures or deformities using pins or screws anchored outside the body, often facilitating easier adjustments and minimizing surgical trauma. In contrast, internal fixation devices are surgically implanted within the body, such as plates, screws, or rods, providing strong internal support for bone alignment and healing. Both types play crucial roles in orthopedic surgery and trauma management.</p></p>
