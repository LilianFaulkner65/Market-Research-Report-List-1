<p><p>The automotive ball and roller bearings market comprises two primary types: ball bearings and roller bearings. Ball bearings use spherical balls to reduce friction between rotating parts, offering high-speed performance and low torque. They are compact and suitable for lighter loads. Roller bearings utilize cylindrical or tapered rollers, providing higher load capacity and durability, ideal for heavy-duty applications. Both types enhance vehicle efficiency, reduce wear, and improve performance, catering to diverse automotive needs across passenger and commercial vehicles.</p></p>
