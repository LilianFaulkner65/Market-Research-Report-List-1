<p><p>The pervious concrete market can be categorized based on void content and strength characteristics. Low porosity (20% ± 5% voids) pervious concrete offers high strength, making it suitable for applications requiring durability and load-bearing capabilities, such as parking lots and roads. In contrast, high porosity (30% ± 5% voids) pervious concrete provides excellent drainage but is of lower strength, making it ideal for landscaped areas and permeable pavements where water infiltration is prioritized. Each type addresses specific environmental and structural needs.</p></p>
