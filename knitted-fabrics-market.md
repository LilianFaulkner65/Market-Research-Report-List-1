<p><p>The knitted fabrics market comprises two primary types: weft knitted fabrics and warp knitted fabrics. Weft knitted fabrics are created by interlocking loops of yarn horizontally, offering elasticity and comfort, commonly used in T-shirts and sweaters. In contrast, warp knitted fabrics are formed by interlacing yarns vertically, resulting in a more stable structure with reduced stretch, ideal for applications like lingerie and sportswear. Both types cater to diverse consumer needs, driving growth in the textiles industry.</p></p>
