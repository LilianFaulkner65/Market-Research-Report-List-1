<p><ul><li>Traditional Fermentation</li><li>Biomass Fermentation</li><li>Precision Fermentation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Fermentation for the alternative protein market encompasses three main types: Traditional Fermentation, Biomass Fermentation, and Precision Fermentation. Traditional Fermentation uses microorganisms to convert sugars into proteins, often seen in products like tempeh and miso. Biomass Fermentation focuses on cultivating microbial biomass, such as fungal proteins, to produce high-protein food sources. Precision Fermentation utilizes engineered microbes to synthesize specific proteins, including those found in dairy and meat, offering tailored nutritional profiles and sustainable production methods for alternative proteins.</p></p>
