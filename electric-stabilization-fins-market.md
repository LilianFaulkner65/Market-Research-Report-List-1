<p><p>Electric stabilization fins, used in marine vessels for stability and maneuverability, come in two main types: Flat Fins and Vector Fins. Flat Fins are simple, fixed structures that provide basic stabilization by reducing roll motion. They are cost-effective and low-maintenance, suitable for smaller or less demanding applications. Vector Fins, on the other hand, are adjustable and can rotate to optimize performance in varying conditions, offering enhanced control and efficiency. They are ideal for larger vessels or those requiring precise stabilization in dynamic environments. Both types cater to different market needs based on vessel size, operational complexity, and budget.</p></p>
