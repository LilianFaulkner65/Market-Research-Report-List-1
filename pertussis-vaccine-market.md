<p><p>The pertussis vaccine market comprises two main types: whole-cell vaccines and acellular vaccines. Whole-cell vaccines contain inactivated bacteria, providing broader immunity and a stronger immune response. They are often associated with more side effects. Acellular vaccines, on the other hand, contain purified components of the bacteria, resulting in fewer side effects and improved safety profiles. While acellular vaccines are more commonly used today, the choice between them often depends on regional health policies and vaccination strategies.</p></p>
