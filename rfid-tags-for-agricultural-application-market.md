<p><p>RFID tags in agriculture are vital for tracking and managing assets. Active RFID tags contain a battery and can transmit data over longer distances, making them ideal for monitoring livestock or high-value equipment. In contrast, passive RFID tags rely on external readers for power and are cost-effective for tracking inventory such as seeds or crops. Both types streamline operations, enhance traceability, and improve efficiency, driving innovations in precision farming and supply chain management.</p></p>
