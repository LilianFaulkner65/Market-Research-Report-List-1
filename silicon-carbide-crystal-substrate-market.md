<p><p>Silicon Carbide (SiC) crystal substrates are primarily categorized into three market types: conductive, semi-insulated, and fully insulated. Conductive SiC substrates are used for high-power and high-frequency applications due to their excellent electrical properties. Semi-insulated substrates, on the other hand, offer reduced conductivity, making them ideal for device isolation and minimizing parasitic capacitance in integrated circuits. This diversity in substrate types caters to various applications, including power electronics, RF devices, and optoelectronics, enhancing performance and efficiency across sectors.</p></p>
