<p><p>The cervical cancer diagnostic market includes various testing methods crucial for early detection. Pap testing involves examining cervical cells for abnormalities. HPV testing detects high-risk human papillomavirus strains linked to cancer. Colposcopy is a visual examination of the cervix, often followed by cervical biopsies to obtain tissue samples for further analysis. Cystoscopy is used primarily to inspect the bladder and urethra, sometimes relevant in evaluating cancer spread. Together, these methods enhance diagnosis and guide treatment strategies.</p></p>
