<p><p>The 30% glass-filled nylon market is primarily categorized into two processing types: injection molding and extrusion molding. Injection molding involves injecting molten nylon mixed with glass fibers into a mold, allowing for complex shapes and high precision, ideal for automotive and industrial parts. Extrusion molding, on the other hand, involves forcing the nylon mixture through a die to create continuous shapes like rods and sheets, commonly used in construction and consumer goods. Both methods enhance the material's strength and thermal resistance.</p></p>
