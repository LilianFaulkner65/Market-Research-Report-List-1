<p><p>The food pesticide residue testing equipment market is categorized into various analytical methods. Gas Chromatography (GC) detects volatile compounds, while Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (GCMS) provides compound identification through mass analysis. For complex residues, GCMSMS enhances detection sensitivity. Liquid Chromatography (LC) is employed for non-volatile substances, with Liquid Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (LCMS) offering detailed analysis. LCMSMS further improves detection limits and specificity, making these technologies essential for ensuring food safety and compliance with regulatory standards regarding pesticide residues.</p></p>
