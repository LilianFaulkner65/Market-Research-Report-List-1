DC relays operate on a steady voltage and are commonly used in battery-powered devices, automotive, and renewable energy applications. In contrast, AC relays are designed for applications that use alternating current, making them suitable for household appliances, industrial machines, and lighting systems. Both types play crucial roles in controlling electrical circuits, enhancing safety and efficiency in various settings.</p></p>
